TOP CHRISTMAS QUOTES AND RIDDLES THAT WILL INSPIRE YOU

Learning never stops. Get inspired with some Christmas quotes and riddles that will sharpen your thinking capacity. Don't just celebrate alone, Celebrating and learning is the best.

Christmas Quotes

1. “Joy to the world, the Lord is come, let earth receive her King.” – “Joy to the World”

2. “Walking in a winter wonderland.” – “Winter Wonderland” 

3. “Peace on earth will come to stay, when we live Christmas every day.” — Helen Steiner Rice 

4. “The way you spend Christmas is far more important than how much.” — Henry David Thoreau

5. “The best and most beautiful things in the world cannot be seen or even touched. They must be felt with the heart.” — Helen Keller

6. “Christmas will always be as long as we stand heart to heart and hand in hand.” — Dr. Seuss

7. “Your children need your presence more than your presents.” — Jesse Jackson

8. “‘One can never have enough socks,’ said Dumbledore. ‘Another Christmas has come and gone and I didn’t get a single pair. People will insist on giving me books.’” — J. K. Rowling

9. “And so this is Christmas… what have you done?” — John Lennon

10. “Christmas isn’t a season. It’s a feeling.” — Edna Ferber

11. “A joy that is shared is a joy made double.” — John Roy

12. “Christmas works like glue. It keeps us all sticking together.” — Rosie Thomas

13. “I’m dreaming of a white Christmas, just like the ones I used to know.”  — Irving Berlin

14. “Love the giver more than the gift.” — Brigham Young

15. “Remembrance, like a candle, burns brightest at Christmastime.” — Charles Dickens

16. “Christmas is doing a little something extra for someone.” — Charles M. Schulz

17. “Christmas is the season for kindling the fire of hospitality.” – Washington Irving

18. “I like to compare the holiday season with the way a child listens to a favorite story. The pleasure is in the familiar way the story begins, the anticipation of familiar turns it takes, the familiar moments of suspense, and the familiar climax and ending.” – Fred Rogers

19. “T’was the night before Christmas, when all through the house, not a creature was stirring, not even a mouse.” – Clement Clarke Moore

20. “When we recall Christmas past, we usually find that the simplest things – not the great occasions – give off the greatest glow of happiness.” – Bob Hope

21. “What is Christmas? It is tenderness for the past, courage for the present, hope for the future.” – Agnes M. Pahro

22. “Thanks be unto God for his unspeakable Gift.” – 2 Corinthians 9:15
